Research Description

The core expertise of the group involves the design, production and purification of recombinant proteins, specifically antibody fragments. We have libraries available that can be used for different applications.

DNA design
Protein production and purification

The specific kind of antibody fragment tht we use is in the form of VHH. This is derived from in vitro engineering natural antibodies from Lamas. Pools of about billions of different VHH sequences are introduced into phage display libraries for convenient and flexible screening.

The in vitro selection from such libraries can be easily performed on any antigen of choice, from whole cells to purified proteins.

VHH have many useful features: they are extremely stable, easy and cheap to produce as a ready to use reagents.

Active Projects
Toxic Algae

Tiny unicellular algae can pose great danger due to their ability to produce toxins. When they are growing in great numbers they can be the cause of harmful algal bloom (HAB).
Antibodies can foster the understanding of their biology and the possibility for detection.
