Reagents for microalgae

Unicellular algae can pose great danger to human health due to their ability to produce toxins. When they are growing in great numbers (bloom) they can be the cause of harmful algal bloom (HAB).

The understanding of algal biology and the monitoring of their blooms are great challenges that can be addressed from different perspectives.

However the availability dedicated to the study and detection of planktonic organisms is limited.

Not to mention that cheap and reliable antibodies are not available.

For this reason unicellular algae are the perfect target in the development of new strategies based on recombinant nanobodies.
